Dell — Windows Quick Guides

Six essential guides for your new Harvard Dell device running Windows 11.

📞

Getting Help from HUIT

Hardware issues, software problems, account help — we've got you.

  • 1
    Submit a ticket online
    Go to huit.harvard.edu/it-help and click "Get Help." Sign in with your HarvardKey. Describe your issue and submit — a technician will follow up.
  • 2
    Call the Help Desk
    Phone: 617-495-7777. Available Monday–Friday, 8am–8pm ET. For urgent issues, call directly.
  • 3
    Walk-in support
    Visit the HUIT Tech Bar in person for hardware repairs, replacements, or hands-on help. Check huit.harvard.edu/locations for current hours and locations.
  • 4
    Loaner devices
    Need a temporary device while yours is serviced? Visit huit.harvard.edu/loaner or ask at the Tech Bar. The WISC program also offers equipment loans.
Tip: When contacting support, have your Harvard ID (HUID) and your device serial number ready. Find the serial number on the label on the bottom of your Dell.
🔄

Restart, Shut Down & Reset

What to do when things slow down, freeze, or need a fresh start.

  • 1
    Restart your device
    Click the Start menu (Windows key) → Power icon → Restart. Do this regularly to apply updates and clear temporary files.
  • 2
    Shut down properly
    Always use Start → Power → Shut Down rather than holding the power button. This ensures Windows saves your settings correctly.
  • 3
    Force restart (frozen device)
    Hold the power button for 10 seconds until the screen goes black. Wait 5 seconds, then press the power button once to restart.
  • 4
    Factory reset (last resort)
    Go to Settings → System → Recovery → Reset this PC. Contact HUIT before doing this — they can recover your data and reconfigure your device properly.
Note: Do not factory reset without contacting HUIT first. Your device is managed by Harvard IT and a reset without support may require re-enrollment.

Saving & Syncing Files

Keep your work safe and accessible from anywhere.

  • 1
    Use OneDrive (recommended)
    Your Harvard Microsoft 365 account includes OneDrive with 1 TB of storage. Save files to your OneDrive folder and they sync automatically to the cloud.
  • 2
    Sign in to OneDrive
    Open OneDrive from the taskbar (cloud icon). Sign in with your Harvard email (e.g. jsmith@harvard.edu). Your files will begin syncing immediately.
  • 3
    SharePoint & Teams files
    Files shared with you in Teams or SharePoint can also sync to your desktop via OneDrive. Right-click any SharePoint folder → Sync.
  • 4
    Never save only to Desktop or Downloads
    These folders are not automatically backed up. Always save important files to OneDrive so you don't lose work if your device needs to be replaced.
Quick check: Look for the OneDrive cloud icon in your taskbar. A green checkmark means your files are synced. A blue spinning icon means syncing is in progress.

Charging & Device Care

Keep your Dell running at its best.

  • 1
    Use the included charger only
    Always use the Dell-supplied USB-C charger or an approved 65W+ USB-C PD adapter. Third-party chargers may underperform or damage the battery.
  • 2
    Battery care tips
    Avoid letting the battery drop below 20% regularly. Dell's battery health software will alert you if the battery needs attention — don't ignore these alerts.
  • 3
    Physical care
    Clean the screen with a dry microfiber cloth only. Avoid eating or drinking near the keyboard. Keep vents on the bottom clear — don't use on soft surfaces like beds or pillows.
  • 4
    Report damage immediately
    If your device is dropped, cracked, or liquid-damaged, contact HUIT right away at 617-495-7777. Prompt reporting may prevent further damage and protect you from liability.
Important: This is Harvard property. Do not attempt to open, repair, or modify the device yourself. All hardware service must go through HUIT.

If Your Device Freezes

Step-by-step recovery when things stop responding.

  • 1
    Wait 60 seconds first
    Sometimes Windows is processing a background update or large task. Give it a full minute before taking action.
  • 2
    Open Task Manager
    Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc. Find the unresponsive app, click it, then click End Task. This closes the frozen application without restarting everything.
  • 3
    Full screen freeze
    Press Ctrl + Alt + Del → choose Restart. If nothing responds, hold the power button for 10 seconds to force shut down.
  • 4
    Still happening after restart?
    If freezing is recurring, contact HUIT support. Don't ignore repeat freezes — they may indicate a hardware issue, failing update, or software conflict that needs attention.

Access & Security

HarvardKey, Okta Verify, and keeping your account safe.

  • 1
    HarvardKey is your master login
    Your HarvardKey (e.g. jsmith) and password unlocks email, Canvas, VPN, and most Harvard systems. Set it up or reset it at key.harvard.edu.
  • 2
    Set up Okta Verify (MFA)
    Harvard requires two-factor authentication. Download Okta Verify on your phone from the App Store or Google Play. Open it, tap Add Account, and scan the QR code shown at login.
  • 3
    Lock your screen when away
    Press Win+L every time you step away from your desk. This is a Harvard IT policy requirement and protects sensitive data.
  • 4
    Report suspicious activity immediately
    If you receive unexpected MFA prompts, notice unfamiliar logins, or think your account is compromised — call HUIT immediately at 617-495-7777. Do not wait.
Never share your HarvardKey password — not with IT support, managers, or colleagues. HUIT will never ask for your password. Anyone who does is attempting fraud.

Apple — Mac Quick Guides

Six essential guides for your new Harvard MacBook or Mac desktop running macOS.

📞

Getting Help from HUIT

Hardware issues, software problems, account help — we've got you.

  • 1
    Submit a ticket online
    Go to huit.harvard.edu/it-help and click "Get Help." Sign in with your HarvardKey. Describe your issue — a technician will follow up via email or phone.
  • 2
    Call the Help Desk
    Phone: 617-495-7777. Available Monday–Friday, 8am–8pm ET. HUIT technicians are trained on both Mac and Windows — no issue is too small.
  • 3
    Walk-in support
    Visit the HUIT Tech Bar for hands-on help with your Mac. They can assist with hardware repairs, Apple ID issues, and software setup. Check huit.harvard.edu/locations.
  • 4
    Loaner devices
    Mac in for repair? Ask about loaner devices at the Tech Bar or visit huit.harvard.edu/loaner. The WISC program also provides equipment access.
Tip: Your serial number is under Apple menu () → About This Mac → System Report. Have your HUID ready when contacting support.
🔄

Restart, Shut Down & Reset

Keep your Mac running smoothly with proper restarts.

  • 1
    Restart your Mac
    Click the Apple menu () → Restart. Restart regularly to install updates and clear memory. Your Mac may prompt you to restart after system updates — don't postpone indefinitely.
  • 2
    Shut down properly
    Apple menu () → Shut Down. This saves your session correctly. Avoid using the power button to shut down unless the Mac is unresponsive.
  • 3
    Force restart (frozen Mac)
    Hold the power button for 10 seconds until the Mac turns off. Wait 5 seconds, then press it once to restart. On MacBooks, the power button is in the top-right corner of the keyboard.
  • 4
    Factory reset (last resort)
    On macOS Ventura and later: Apple menu → System Settings → General → Transfer or Reset → Erase All Content and Settings. Contact HUIT before doing this — your device is Harvard-managed.
Note: Harvard-managed Macs use Jamf for device management. A factory reset without HUIT involvement may require re-enrollment and can delay your work.
☁

Saving & Syncing Files

Keep your work safe and accessible from anywhere.

  • 1
    Use OneDrive (recommended)
    Download OneDrive from the Mac App Store (free with your Harvard account). Sign in with your Harvard email and your files sync to the cloud automatically.
  • 2
    OneDrive in Finder
    Once signed in, a OneDrive folder appears in your Finder sidebar. Save any work file there and it's automatically backed up and accessible from any device.
  • 3
    iCloud is NOT for Harvard work
    Do not save Harvard work files to iCloud Drive — this is a personal Apple service and does not meet Harvard's data security requirements. Use OneDrive for all work documents.
  • 4
    SharePoint & Teams
    Team files in Microsoft Teams and SharePoint can also sync to your Mac via OneDrive. Open any Teams channel → Files tab → click Sync to add them to Finder.
Quick check: The OneDrive icon in your menu bar shows a green checkmark when synced, a spinning icon when syncing, and a red X if there's a problem. Click it to see details.

Charging & Device Care

Keep your Mac in top condition.

  • 1
    Use Apple's charger
    Always use the Apple MagSafe or USB-C charger included with your Mac. For USB-C models, use at least a 67W adapter. Underpowered chargers may not charge under load.
  • 2
    Optimized battery charging
    macOS includes Optimized Battery Charging — it learns your routine and pauses charging above 80% to extend battery lifespan. This is normal and intentional. Check: System Settings → Battery.
  • 3
    Screen and keyboard care
    Use only a dry microfiber cloth on the display — no sprays or liquids. Do not close the MacBook with anything on the keyboard (pens, cables) — this can crack the screen.
  • 4
    Report damage immediately
    Contact HUIT at 617-495-7777 if your Mac is physically damaged. Do not attempt any self-repair — opening an Apple device by a non-authorized technician can void service coverage.
Important: Harvard Macs are covered under AppleCare managed through HUIT. All repairs go through the Tech Bar — do not take your device to a retail Apple Store without HUIT authorization.

If Your Mac Freezes

Step-by-step recovery when apps or the system stop responding.

  • 1
    Wait 60 seconds first
    macOS may be running a background process (Spotlight indexing, software update, Time Machine backup). Give it a full minute before taking action.
  • 2
    Force quit a frozen app
    Press ⌘ + Option + Esc. Select the unresponsive app and click Force Quit. This closes just that app without restarting the whole system.
  • 3
    Full system freeze
    If nothing responds, hold the power button for 10 seconds until the Mac shuts off. Wait 5 seconds, then press the power button to restart.
  • 4
    Still happening after restart?
    Open Activity Monitor (search in Spotlight: ⌘ Space → type "Activity Monitor") to see which processes are consuming the most CPU or Memory. Contact HUIT if the issue persists.

Access & Security

HarvardKey, Okta Verify, and keeping your account safe on Mac.

  • 1
    HarvardKey is your master login
    Your HarvardKey unlocks email, VPN, Canvas, and most Harvard systems. Set up or reset your password at key.harvard.edu. Your Harvard email is separate from your Mac login.
  • 2
    Set up Okta Verify (MFA)
    Download Okta Verify on your phone. Open it → tap Add Account → choose Organization → scan the QR code shown at your next Harvard login. Required for all Harvard systems.
  • 3
    Use Touch ID to lock and unlock
    On MacBooks with Touch ID, set it up under System Settings → Touch ID & Password. Press Ctrl+⌘+Q to lock your screen whenever you step away.
  • 4
    Report suspicious activity immediately
    Unexpected MFA pushes, unfamiliar login alerts, or strange behavior? Call HUIT at 617-495-7777 right away. Do not approve MFA requests you didn't initiate.
Note on Apple ID: Your Harvard Mac may be linked to a Managed Apple ID through Harvard. Do not sign in with your personal Apple ID on a Harvard-managed device unless specifically directed by HUIT.
